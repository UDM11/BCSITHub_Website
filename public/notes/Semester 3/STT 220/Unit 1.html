1.1 Meaning and Definition of Statistics

Statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, summarizing, presenting, analyzing, and interpreting numerical data to draw meaningful conclusions and support decision-making.

Two Main Branches of Statistics

  • Descriptive Statistics
    Summarizes and describes the main features of data (no generalization)
    Tools: Tables, charts, measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode), dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation)
  • Inferential Statistics
    Uses sample data to make generalizations / predictions about a population
    Tools: Hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, regression, probability distributions

Important Definitions

  • Statistics (plural): Numerical data or figures themselves (e.g., "Statistics show unemployment rose to 11.4%")
  • Statistic (singular): A numerical value calculated from sample data (e.g., sample mean = 72.5)
  • Parameter: Numerical value describing a population (e.g., population mean μ)
  • Population: Entire group of interest (e.g., all students of Tribhuvan University)
  • Sample: Subset of population selected for study
Simple Nepali context example:
Descriptive: “In 2081 BS, average marks of 100 BCA students was 68.4”
Inferential: “Based on this sample, we can say average marks of all BCA students in Nepal is between 66–70 with 95% confidence”

1.3 Variable and its Types

A variable is any characteristic, number, or quantity that can be measured or counted and that varies across individuals, objects, or time.

Main Classification of Variables

Basis Type Definition Examples Scale of Measurement Statistical Operations Allowed
Qualitative / Categorical Nominal Categories with no order or ranking Gender (M/F/O), Blood group (A/B/AB/O), Favorite color Nominal Mode, frequency, chi-square test
Ordinal Categories with natural order/ranking but unequal intervals Education level (SLC, +2, Bachelor, Master), Satisfaction (Very poor, Poor, Average, Good, Excellent) Ordinal Median, mode, rank correlation
Quantitative / Numerical Discrete Countable whole numbers (finite or countably infinite) Number of students in class, number of bugs in code, page views Interval or Ratio All arithmetic operations, mean, standard deviation
Continuous Any value within a range (measurable) Height, weight, CPU usage %, download speed (Mbps), time to load webpage Interval or Ratio Mean, median, standard deviation, correlation, regression
Quick memory trick:
Nominal → Names only
Ordinal → Order matters
Discrete → Countable jumps
Continuous → Can take any value (infinite decimals)

1.4 Limitation of Statistics

Statistics is a very powerful tool but has several limitations that must be understood to avoid misuse.

  • Deals only with quantitative data
    Cannot directly measure qualitative aspects (love, morale, beauty, honesty)
  • Results are true only on average
    “Average marks = 68” does not mean every student scored 68
  • Can be misused / misinterpreted
    “Lies, damned lies, and statistics” (Mark Twain) – cherry-picking, wrong averages, manipulated graphs
  • Requires large & representative samples
    Small or biased samples → wrong conclusions (e.g., non-random online poll)
  • Does not explain cause-and-effect
    Correlation ≠ causation (ice cream sales & drowning incidents both rise in summer)
  • Future predictions are uncertain
    Based on past data → cannot account for black swan events
  • Quality depends on data quality
    Garbage In → Garbage Out (inaccurate data → misleading results)
  • Does not provide solutions
    Only tells “what is happening” — not “what should be done”
Common misuse in Nepal:
• Political parties showing “average approval rating” from biased samples
• Job ads claiming “highest salary package” based on one outlier
