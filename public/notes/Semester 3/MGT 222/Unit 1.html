Unit I: Introduction to Management (5 hrs.)

1.9 Concept and Characteristics of Management

Concept of Management

Management is the process of **planning, organizing, leading, and controlling** the efforts of organizational members and resources to achieve specific goals efficiently and effectively.

Key Definitions (popular views):

  • Henri Fayol (Father of Modern Management): “To manage is to forecast and plan, to organize, to command, to co-ordinate and to control.”
  • Mary Parker Follett: “The art of getting things done through people.”
  • Peter Drucker: “Management is a multi-purpose organ that manages business, manages managers and manages workers and work.”
  • Modern view (2020s): Management is the coordination of all resources (human, financial, physical, informational) through planning, organizing, leading, and controlling to achieve organizational objectives in a dynamic environment.

Characteristics / Nature of Management

CharacteristicExplanation
Goal-orientedEvery management activity is directed toward achieving specific objectives
UniversalManagement principles apply to all types of organizations (business, government, NGO, hospital, school…)
MultidisciplinaryDraws knowledge from psychology, sociology, economics, mathematics, statistics, IT, etc.

Management is both **science** (principles, theories) and **art** (application depends on manager’s skill, creativity, judgment).

1.10 Basic Management Functions

Henri Fayol identified five functions; modern textbooks (especially in Nepal & India) commonly use **four** or **five** functions.

Four Basic Functions (POCCC / POLC)

  1. Planning: Setting objectives and deciding how to achieve them
    • Types: Strategic, Tactical, Operational
    • Tools: SWOT, PESTEL, Forecasting
  2. Organizing: Arranging resources and tasks to achieve objectives
    • Structure: Departmentalization, Delegation, Authority & Responsibility
  3. Leading / Directing: Motivating, guiding, and influencing people
    • Leadership styles: Autocratic, Democratic, Laissez-faire
    • Motivation theories: Maslow, Herzberg, Vroom
  4. Controlling: Monitoring performance and taking corrective action
    • Steps: Set standards → Measure performance → Compare → Correct
Some textbooks add **Staffing** or **Coordinating** as fifth function. In TU exams, usually four functions (Planning, Organizing, Leading, Controlling) are expected.

Mintzberg emphasized that managers perform all roles simultaneously — not in sequence.

1.12 Fundamental Management Skills

Robert Katz (1955) classified three types of skills required at different management levels.

LevelTechnical SkillsHuman SkillsConceptual SkillsDominance
Top Level (CEO, Directors)LowMediumHighConceptual dominant
Middle Level (Department Heads)MediumHighMedium-HighHuman dominant
Lower / First-Line (Supervisors)HighHighLowTechnical + Human dominant
  • Technical Skills: Knowledge of tools, processes, procedures (e.g., accounting, coding, machine operation)
  • Human / Interpersonal Skills: Ability to work with people, motivate, communicate, resolve conflict
  • Conceptual Skills: Ability to see organization as whole, think strategically, understand complex situations
Modern addition (2020s): Digital skills, emotional intelligence (EQ), adaptability, and data-driven decision-making are now considered essential across all levels.

1.13 Contemporary Issues of Management

Modern managers face new challenges due to globalization, technology, changing workforce, and societal expectations.

Major Contemporary Issues (2025 context)

  • Globalization & International Management: Cross-cultural teams, global supply chains, outsourcing
  • Digital Transformation & Technology: AI, automation, big data, cybersecurity threats
  • Remote & Hybrid Work: Managing virtual teams, work-life balance, digital fatigue
  • Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI): Gender, ethnicity, generational diversity, inclusive leadership
  • Sustainability & ESG: Environmental responsibility, carbon footprint, ethical sourcing
  • Changing Workforce Expectations: Gig economy, Gen Z values, mental health, flexible work
  • Ethical & Corporate Governance Issues: Corruption, CSR, whistleblowing, data privacy (GDPR-like laws)
  • Agile & Adaptive Management: Fast-changing environment → need for agility, innovation
  • Talent Management & Employee Retention: High turnover, skill gap, employer branding
In TU exams, contemporary issues often focus on: globalization, technology/AI impact, sustainability, remote work, and diversity.
